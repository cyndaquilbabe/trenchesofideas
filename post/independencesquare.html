They Couldn't Convince Congress

It's always an adventure in Philadelphia, more so when you can't find where you parked and it's cold outside. Or when you're grandma is so relieved to be away from your extended family that it's not even funny, it's just sad.

So disregarding my extended family's douchebaggery - Independence Square! It;s huge and sort of, kind of handicap accessible if you're alright being separated from your group to go through the exit into the buildings. It was stressful because my mom went with my sister that way, and me and my grandmother went the usual way - where the metal detectors are. Trying to strip off my jacket walk through and then put it back on fast enough to get out of the way was a trip and a half - but that's all security for you.

Bright side! Front of the line! Down side, I had no idea what was going on and neither did my grandmother and so the beginning was very stressful. The buildings were cool and the presentation on the drafting of the Declaration of Independence was awesome.

I don't know what all to say, I really love Philadelphia and the Independence Square - if you ever get option to visit, I highkey recommend it.
