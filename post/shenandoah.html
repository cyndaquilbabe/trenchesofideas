Drive Along That Skyline Drive

Scenic views are what most came for, but I left with a lot of bear sighting jokes instead. Go figure. Also pokemon hunting doesn't work well when your phone has no signal and there's fog everywhere.

To start with, it was me, my aunt, and my two younger cousins - 17 and 9 years old respectively. I kept my baby cousin entertained while my aunt and other cousin navigated because Lord knows Google likes to mess with us all the time. As we started driving up and up and up, we stopped at overlooks and then, of course, we spotted the bear warning sign.

The first thing that you need to know about my aunt is that she's very scared of a lot of things and tries to control her children's actions because of it. It didn't work out because I'm a corrupting influence. I mean, who's going to see a bear in the middle of winter anyway? There's this thing called hibernation that bears do as far as I know? Next, I need you to know that I can take things to far and to prove it I started saying in a mockery of a video I've seen somewhere, "a beAR." To get the full effect, I need you to imagine saying that in a russian accent at full volume and then being echoed by your favorite cousins in the same false accent and volume.

That, uh, that pretty much sums up this national park visit to be honest. Shouting "a bear" at full volume while ascending into thick fog the further you go in.
